# Link Building Campaign Velocity Targets

## Why Velocity Targeting Matters

Search algorithms are sensitive to link acquisition patterns. A sudden surge from a new campaign, even if every acquired link is high quality, can register as anomalous if it dramatically outpaces the site's historical acquisition rate. Velocity targeting ensures that campaign activity expands the backlink profile at a rate that appears organic relative to the site's established history and competitive context.

## Calculating Your Velocity Target

Start with the site's organic baseline: average new linking root domains per month over the preceding 12 months, excluding any documented spike events. This is the floor of the velocity targetâ€”any campaign should sustain at least this baseline rate.

Next, establish the competitive gap: how many more root domains per month are leading competitors in the target SERP acquiring compared to the target site? The velocity target should be set to close this gap within a realistic timeframe (typically 12 to 24 months) rather than attempting to match it immediately.

**Example calculation:**
- Site baseline: 8 new root domains/month
- Top competitor acquisition rate: 20 new root domains/month
- Gap: 12 root domains/month
- Target: 14â€“16 new root domains/month (50â€“100% above baseline, moving toward competitive parity over 12 months)

## Quality Constraints on Velocity Targets

Velocity targets are bounded by quality standards. Acquiring 20 new root domains per month from low-authority sources to meet a numerical target produces worse outcomes than acquiring 10 per month from high-authority editorial sources. Set minimum quality thresholds (e.g., DA 30+ or equivalent) as a constraint on velocity target fulfillment.

## Campaign Pacing Strategies

Rather than front-loading link acquisitionâ€”common in agencies pursuing rapid resultsâ€”distribute campaign links across the campaign period at a consistent weekly rate. A campaign delivering four to five new links per week produces a smoother velocity profile than one delivering zero links for five weeks followed by a twenty-link burst.

## Adjusting Targets After Algorithm Updates

After major Google algorithm updates, reassess velocity targets against updated competitive landscape data. Updates frequently reshuffle competitive link profiles in ways that change both the velocity gap and the quality distribution benchmarks against which the target site should be calibrated. Post-update velocity recalibration ensures campaign targets remain grounded in current competitive reality.
