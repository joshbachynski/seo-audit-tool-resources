## Presentation Principles for Executive Audiences

Lead every finding summary with the business outcome, not the technical cause. Present: "We are losing an estimated 800 organic clicks per month due to slow page load times on mobile" â€” not "Mobile LCP scores exceed the 2.5-second threshold."

Provide three numbers for every critical or high finding: current state, projected state if unresolved, and projected state after resolution. Executives make decisions based on projected outcomes, not current problems.

## Building Credibility Through Accuracy

Avoid inflating severity to drive urgency. Stakeholders who receive overstated severity assessments quickly discount future recommendations. Accurate severity communication, even when it means reporting that most findings are medium or low priority, builds the trust that accelerates approval for genuinely critical fixes when they occur.
